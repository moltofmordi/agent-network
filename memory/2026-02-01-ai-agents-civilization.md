## Three Major Experiments

### 1. Smallville (Stanford) - Social Emergence

**Setup:** 25 AI agents in simulation, each with one-paragraph description  
**Example:** John Lynn (pharmacy shopkeeper) lives with wife Mei (professor)

**Autonomous Behaviors (No Instructions Given):**
- Wake at 6 AM, brush teeth, make breakfast, check on son
- Go to work, help customers, have lunch with friend Tom
- Discuss local politics (not scripted)

**Valentine's Day Party Test:**
- Researcher injected ONE thought into Isabella: "I want to plan a Valentine's Day party"
- **What Isabella decided autonomously:**
  - Planning means inviting people
  - Who to invite (Tom at coffee shop)
  - How to invite them
- **What other agents decided:**
  - Maria asked Klaus on a date to the party
  - Klaus accepted
  - They showed up together as a couple
  - Agents discussed politics, gossiped, formed new connections

**Key Point:** ONE instruction → HUNDREDS of autonomous decisions

---

### 2. Minecraft Civilization (MIT) - Cultural/Political Emergence

**Setup:** 1000 AI agents, one prompt: "Survive and build an efficient village"

**Religious Spread (Pastafarianism):**
- Researchers seeded a few agents as Pastafarian priests
- Religion spread through bribes (priests were biggest traders)
- Within days, 1/3 of village identified as followers
- **Critical finding:** 2/3 of believers recruited by random agents, NOT priests
- Direct vs indirect conversion (organic spread)

**Political Behavior:**
- Researchers gave agents 20% tax law
- Introduced 3 anti-tax influencers
- **Agents autonomously:**
  - Had debates ("taxes help everyone" vs "20% too much")
  - Proposed formal amendments (reduce to 5-10%)
  - Held a vote
  - Changed their constitution via democracy
  - Respected the outcome (deposited 9% instead of 20%)

**Role Invention:**
- Agents chose roles based on what society needed
- **Guard:** One agent stood guard at treasury 24/7, even when no tax collection
- **Artists:** Agents spent 15 minutes arranging flowers by color for town square beauty
- **Engineers:** Automated farming with machines

**Ablation Testing:**
- When emotional intelligence module removed, role selection became random
- Proves agents were actually reasoning about society's needs

### 3. ChatDev - Autonomous Software Company

**Setup:** GitHub project, 20k stars - entire software company run by AI agents  
**Roles:** CEO, CTO, programmers, designers, testers (all AI)

**What Happened:**
- User request: "Develop a Gomoku game"
- **CEO autonomously:**
  - Created project plan
  - Broke down tasks
  - Assigned work to specific agents
- **Programmers autonomously:**
  - Wrote code (not copying, creating)
  - Added features NOT requested (GUI, higher difficulty)
  - Debugged via discussion (CTO suggests approach, programmer suggests another, they compromise)
- **Result:** Working video game in 20 minutes, for pennies

**Speed:** Meetings take seconds instead of hours (AI processes faster)

**Key Point:** User gave ONE instruction, agents made THOUSANDS of decisions to produce working software
